This paper investigates the ferromagnetic phase transition properties of nanoislands with decorated lattice using the correlated effective field theory based on the transverse-field Ising model. The findings demonstrate that the surface atoms predominantly determine the unique phase transition properties, while the transverse field and decorating exchange interaction modulate the specific details, including the reentrant phenomenon.
