I am an AI engineer working on large language models and AI agents at China Mobile. I received my master's degree from the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC), where my research focused on quantum machine learning, hidden quantum Markov models (HQMM), and financial time-series analysis.

My current work focuses on building enterprise-level AI agent systems and applying cutting-edge large language models to industrial systems. It also involves constructing an AI MaaS infrastructure for deploying and fine-tuning the latest open-source models. During graduate school, I mainly focused on quantum computing and fundamental physics.

Currently pursuing Ph.D. preparation in LLMs and AI Agents, I am actively seeking a full-time Research Assistant role to fill my academic and research experience gap. I leverage my solid technical and theoretical expertise in AI and AI Agent research to accelerate experimental iteration, model optimization and project progress, while accumulating systematic research experience to underpin my upcoming Ph.D. applications and deliver consistent research contributions to the team.

This pursuit is driven by both genuine curiosity and practical reflection. In my daily work, I have hands-on experience deploying LLMs and building agent systems across real-world industrial scenarios — from model serving and optimization to system integration. These projects have given me a solid foundation in applied AI. At the same time, they have sparked a deeper curiosity: I want to go beyond application-level work and push into the underlying architecture design, training optimization, and algorithmic innovation. Pursuing a Ph.D. would allow me to develop that deeper expertise and contribute at a more fundamental level.
